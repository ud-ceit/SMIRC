About the Conference

The Student Multidisciplinary Innovation and Research Conference (SMIRC) is an annual academic platform that highlights the creativity, innovation, and research excellence of undergraduate and graduate students across diverse disciplines.

The conference brings together students, faculty, researchers, and industry professionals to explore the intersections between Computing and Information Systems, Engineering, Business, Law, and Education. Each edition of SMIRC includes two sessions per year, organized at the end of each academic semester (Fall and Spring), offering continuous opportunities for students to present their work, exchange ideas, and engage in interdisciplinary collaboration.

This year, under the theme "Innovating Across Disciplines for a Smarter Future," SMIRC promotes cross-disciplinary research, applied innovation, and problem-solving that address contemporary societal and technological challenges. Through poster sessions and live demonstrations, participants will showcase projects that reflect emerging trends in artificial intelligence, sustainability, digital transformation, and data-driven decision-making.

The conference also features expert panels and innovation challenges led by academic and industry leaders. These sessions aim to nurture a culture of creativity, strengthen university-industry partnerships, and align student research with national priorities in artificial intelligence, sustainability, and smart development.

Sponsored by the Research Office, in collaboration with the IEEE UAE Section and participating colleges, SMIRC seeks to inspire a culture of inquiry, collaboration, and lifelong learning across all academic disciplines.

Submission Instructions

Submission Link

Submit your papers and abstracts using the online form: SMIRC 2026 Submission Portal

Submission Guidelines

  • Submissions can include research papers, posters, innovation projects, or demonstrations.
  • All papers must be original and unpublished work by students (faculty co-authorship is allowed for guidance).
  • Papers should follow the IEEE conference format (4 pages).
  • Each submission will undergo peer review by the conference committee.
  • All submissions must be written in English.
  • Only PDF files will be accepted for the review process.
  • All submissions must be made through EDAS.

Publication and Indexing

Accepted papers will be submitted for possible inclusion into IEEE Xplore subject to meeting IEEE Xplore's scope and quality requirements, and to be indexed in Scopus and Clarivate SCI indexing (IEEE LoA#69786).

Accepted papers, posters, and demonstrations will be showcased during SMIRC 2026 as part of the poster and demonstration sessions.

Selected works may be considered for publication in the University of Dubai's Research Repository.

Formatting and Requirements

  • All submissions must be written in English.
  • The maximum paper length is four to six (4-6) printed pages (10-point font) in the standard IEEE two-column conference format.
  • Two extra pages are allowed at an additional cost of $100 per page.
  • Papers exceeding (6+2) pages or less than four pages will not be accepted on EDAS.

Templates

Standard IEEE conference templates for LaTeX formats are found at: IEEE Templates

You may also use one of the following templates for Microsoft Word: A4, US letter.

Submission Process

  • Only PDF files will be accepted for the review process.
  • All submissions must be made through EDAS.
  • If you have any questions regarding the submission of manuscripts, please contact the conference chairs via email.

Plagiarism and AI Policy

Similarity in the submitted paper must be less than 30%. Submissions exceeding this threshold will be rejected.

Authors must declare the use of AI in the preparation of their manuscript, including the role of AI in generating content, analysis, or any other contributions. For more details, please refer to the IEEE policy for AI usage.
