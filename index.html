Welcome

The Student Multidisciplinary Innovation and Research Conference (SMIRC) is an annual academic platform that highlights the creativity, innovation, and research excellence of undergraduate and graduate students across diverse disciplines. The conference brings together students, faculty, researchers, and industry professionals to explore the intersections between Computing and Information Systems, Engineering, Business, Law, and Education. Each edition of SMIRC includes two sessions per year, organized at the end of each academic semester (Fall and Spring), offering continuous opportunities for students to present their work, exchange ideas, and engage in interdisciplinary collaboration.

This year, under the theme ‚ÄúInnovating Across Disciplines for a Smarter Future,‚Äù SMIRC promotes cross-disciplinary research, applied innovation, and problem-solving that address contemporary societal and technological challenges. Through poster sessions, and live demonstrations, participants will showcase projects that reflect emerging trends in artificial intelligence, sustainability, digital transformation, and data-driven decision-making.
The conference also features expert panels and innovation challenges led by academic and industry leaders. These sessions aim to nurture a culture of creativity, strengthen university‚Äìindustry partnerships, and align student research with national priorities in artificial intelligence, sustainability, and smart development. Sponsored by the Research Office, in collaboration with the IEEE UAE Section and participating colleges, SMIRC seeks to inspire a culture of inquiry, collaboration, and lifelong learning across all academic disciplines.

The first session of SMIRC 2025 will be held on November 29, 2025, at the Academic City Campus, Dubai.

Call For Papers

Submissions are invited from undergraduate and graduate students across all disciplines, including but not limited to:

  • Computing and Information Systems: Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, Cybersecurity, Cloud Computing, Internet of Things, and Smart Applications.
  • Engineering and Technology: Robotics, Renewable Energy, Embedded Systems, Smart Cities, and Sustainable Design.
  • Business and Management: Innovation Management, Digital Transformation, Entrepreneurship, and FinTech.
  • Law and Policy: Cyber Law, Digital Governance, Data Privacy, and Legal Aspects of Emerging Technologies.
  • Education and Social Sciences: Technology-Enhanced Learning, Educational Innovation, and Social Impact Research.

Paper Submission Instructions

  • Submissions can include research papers, posters, innovation projects, or demonstrations.
  • All papers must be original and unpublished work by students (faculty co-authorship is allowed for guidance).
  • Papers should follow the IEEE conference format (4 pages).
  • Each submission will undergo peer review by the conference committee.
  • Accepted papers, posters, and demonstrations will be showcased during SMIRC 2025 as part of the poster and demonstration sessions.
  • Selected works may be considered for publication in the University of Dubai‚Äôs Research Repository.
